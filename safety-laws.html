Professional Safety Guidelines

Written by Mahmodul Hasan

1. General Occupational Safety

  • Hazard assessments must be conducted before starting any job.
  • Use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is mandatory.
  • Toolbox meetings should be held daily before starting work.
  • Even minor risks must not be ignored.
  • Every worker is also responsible for their own safety.
  • Regular safety observations are necessary.
  • Reporting near misses is part of a strong safety culture.
  • Proper training is mandatory before entering any site.
  • Work permits are required for each task.
  • All signboards must be visible and clean.

2. Fire Safety

  • Every second counts during a fire.
  • Fire extinguishers must always be ready and accessible.
  • Everyone should be trained on how to use fire extinguishers.
  • Hot work requires a proper work permit.
  • A fire watcher must be assigned during hot work.
  • Fire alarm systems must be functional at all times.
  • Evacuation paths must be kept clear.
  • All personnel should be trained on fire response procedures.
  • Fire drills must be conducted at least once per month.
  • Flammable materials should be stored in secured locations.

3. Working at Height Safety

  • Fall protection is mandatory for work above 1.8 meters.
  • Harness use is compulsory and must be inspected regularly.
  • Life lines must be used for added safety.
  • Maintain three-point contact when using ladders.
  • Do not use ladders that are wet or oily.
  • Check the “Green Tag” before using scaffolding.
  • Untrained individuals must not climb scaffolds.
  • Never allow work below any suspended objects.
  • Provide nets or barricades when working at height.

4. Electrical Safety

  • Isolate electrical circuits before any work begins.
  • Follow LOTO (Lockout Tagout) procedures strictly.
  • Never handle electrical tools with wet hands.
  • Inspect all power cables before use.
  • Use circuit breakers with proper ratings.
  • Guard all temporary power cables appropriately.
  • Only authorized electricians may perform electrical tasks.
  • Keep control panels clean and accessible.
  • Do not interfere with power connections.
  • Everyone should know the emergency shutdown switch location.

5. Confined Space Safety

  • Conduct a gas test before entering any confined space.
  • Oxygen, LEL, H₂S, and CO readings must be within normal range.
  • Only trained personnel are allowed to enter confined spaces.
  • No entry is allowed without a valid Entry Permit.
  • Maintain constant communication between inside and outside personnel.
  • A watcher must always be present outside the confined space.
  • Harnesses, tripods, and rescue devices must be available and ready.
  • No hot work inside confined spaces without proper authorization.
  • Respiratory protection is required in addition to PPE.
  • Ensure all personnel have exited after task completion.
