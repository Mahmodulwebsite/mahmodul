Professional Safety Guidelines

Written by Mahmodul Hasan

1. General Occupational Safety

  • Hazard assessments must be conducted before starting any job.
  • Use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is mandatory.
  • Toolbox meetings should be held daily before starting work.
  • Even minor risks must not be ignored.
  • Every worker is also responsible for their own safety.
  • Regular safety observations are necessary.
  • Reporting near misses is part of a strong safety culture.
  • Proper training is mandatory before entering any site.
  • Work permits are required for each task.
  • All signboards must be visible and clean.

2. Fire Safety

  • Every second counts during a fire.
  • Fire extinguishers must always be ready and accessible.
  • Everyone should be trained on how to use fire extinguishers.
  • Hot work requires a proper work permit.
  • A fire watcher must be assigned during hot work.
  • Fire alarm systems must be functional at all times.
  • Evacuation paths must be kept clear.

3. Working at Height Safety

  • Fall protection is mandatory when working at or above 1.8 meters.
  • Wearing a harness is compulsory.
  • Harnesses must be regularly inspected.
  • Use lifelines for added safety.
  • Ensure three-point contact while using ladders.
  • Do not use wet or oily ladders.
  • Check scaffold tags before use.
  • Untrained individuals are not allowed on scaffolds.
  • Never work below heavy objects without protection.
  • Use nets or barricades to protect workers below.

4. Electrical Safety

  • Isolate circuits before starting electrical work.
  • Follow the Lockout Tagout (LOTO) procedures.
  • Do not use electrical equipment with wet hands.
  • Inspect all electrical wires before use.
  • Use circuit breakers with correct ratings.
  • Protect temporary cables using guarding systems.
  • Only authorized electricians may perform electrical work.
  • Keep control panels clean and accessible.
  • Never interfere with electrical connections.
  • Know the location of emergency shutdown switches.

5. Confined Space Safety

  • Gas testing must be done before entering confined spaces.
  • Oxygen, LEL, Hâ‚‚S and CO levels must be within safe limits.
  • Only trained personnel are allowed entry.
  • Entry without a valid permit is prohibited.
  • Maintain communication between inside and outside workers.
  • A safety watcher must always be present.
  • Keep harnesses, tripods, and rescue gear ready.
  • Hot work is not allowed unless specifically permitted.
  • Use respiratory protection in addition to PPE.
  • Ensure all workers exit after task completion.
